About Me

My name is Suriana Rodriguez and I am in my third year of the Excelsior Academy P-TECH program. By the time I graduate, I will have received my high school diploma and an Associate's degree in Cyber Security. For the last few years, I have been taking College courses and high school classes at the same time and this has put me in the position to graduate next year, with both degrees. I chose the field of cyber security because I recognize the importance of this field for both professional organizations and individual clients.

I am also dedicated to my community. Aside from teaching structural programming and coding to underserved youth in the Newburgh Armory Unity Center, I am also a founding member of Global to Local and serve on the Excelsior Academy Awareness Committee. Global to Local is a student-led global service learning program in which students fundraise to volunteer overseas and then return to our city to act as Project Managers for local, community-based impact projects. All of these endeavors show my commitment to both social equity and volunteerism.
